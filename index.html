George Washington Carver was a pioneer in environmental science. He is well known for his work with agriculture, inventions and peanuts!

Timeline of GWC's Amazing Life

  1. 1860's - GWC was born into slavery in missouri. He didn't know his own birthday, only that it was sometime just before 1865 when slavery was abolished. His parents were purchased for $700

  2. One Week Old - GWC, his sister and mother were kidnapped or "stolen". George was eventually recovered by his "owner" Moses Carver, and returned to him and was reunited with his brother. His mother and sister were not recovered :( .

  3. Childhood - GWC was taught how to read and write by his "Aunt". He identified himself as "Carver's George"

  4. Still a youngin - GWC tried to go to a school for black children that was 10 miles away, he slept in a barn and eventually rented a room from a woman named Mariah Watkins who said he should call himself "George Carver" and encouraged him to spread the knowledge he would earn.

  5. Teen - George moved around from family to family, often afraid for his life. Eventually he got his diploma from Minneapolis Kansas ( Not the place in minnesota )

  6. Young adulthood - George applied and was accepted to university in kansas. When he got there they said he actually couldn't attend because he was black

  7. Early 20's - George Homesteaded a small parcel of land. He busted his ass on 17 acres and began farming and doing odd jobs, eventually he secured a 300$ educational loan

  8. Student George - GWC started out studying art and piano, eventually he changed gears and studied botany, his research about plant pathology gained recognition and he was the first black faculty member at Iowa State!

  9. Proffesor George - He became the head of the Agriculture at Tuskegee University where he taught for 47 years
